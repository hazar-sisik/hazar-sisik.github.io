Industrial Glass Annealing Furnace Digital Twin

Developed a full-scale digital twin of an industrial glass annealing furnace using real operating conditions, including actual furnace dimensions and production loads. Designed and implemented a custom conjugate heat transfer framework by combining an OpenFOAM-based RANS solver with a custom GPU-accelerated Monte Carlo ray tracing solver in NVIDIA OptiX C++ for surface-to-surface radiation modelling.

Developed both steady-state and transient solvers to capture coupled airflow, wall temperatures, solid conduction, and transient thermal behavior of moving glassware. Built structured blocking topologies and high-quality meshes in ANSYS ICEM CFD for complex glassware geometries, and integrated the complete workflow using Python.

Transferred OpenFOAM and Monte Carlo simulation outputs to ParaView and performed advanced post-processing and scientific visualization, including furnace and glassware temperature fields, airflow and velocity distributions, contour analysis, and extraction of thermal/fluid properties for further engineering interpretation in Python.

The framework was verified with ANSYS Fluent and Star-CCM+ using real plant data, achieved an accuracy of ±3–5 °C across 9 heating and cooling zones, and resulted in an 8% reduction in energy consumption together with improved glassware quality in real operation.
